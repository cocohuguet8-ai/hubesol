## The difference in one table

| | Say | Tell |
|---|---|---|
| Person after the verb? | no (or 'to' + person) | yes — always |
| Example | She **said** she was tired. | She **told me** she was tired. |
| With 'to' | She **said** hello **to** him. | — (never 'told to me') |
| Fixed phrases | say hello, say sorry, say a word | tell the truth, tell a story, tell the time |

The quick test: if a **person** comes straight after the verb, use **tell**; if not, use **say**.

## When to use each

### 1. Tell — with a person

- She **told me** the news.
- **Tell him** to wait.

### 2. Say — without a person (or with 'to')

- He **said** he was busy.
- She **said** goodbye **to** everyone.

### 3. Learn the fixed phrases

- **tell** the truth / a lie / a story / the time
- **say** hello / sorry / nothing / a word

> ⚠️ Typical error: no 'to' after tell, and no person straight after say: *She told me* (not ~~told to me~~); *She said to me* (not ~~said me~~).

## Common mistakes

**1. 'Say me' instead of 'tell me'.** *~~She said me the answer~~* → **She told me the answer.**

**2. 'Tell to me' instead of 'tell me'.** *~~He told to me a story~~* → **He told me a story.**

**3. Wrong fixed phrase.** *~~say the truth~~* → **tell the truth. Pro tip:** person right after the verb → tell; no person → say.
