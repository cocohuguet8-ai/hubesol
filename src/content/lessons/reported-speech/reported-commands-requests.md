## How to report a command or request

Use **reporting verb + object + to-infinitive**.

| Direct speech | Reported speech |
|---|---|
| "Sit down." | He **told me to** sit down. |
| "Please wait." | She **asked me to** wait. |
| "Don't touch that." | He **told me not to** touch that. |
| "Remember to call." | She **reminded me to** call. |
| "Don't be late." | He **warned me not to** be late. |

There is **no backshift** — the to-infinitive stays the same. Always include the person.

## When and how to change things

### 1. Commands — tell

- "Open the window." → She **told me to** open the window.

### 2. Requests — ask

- "Could you help me?" → He **asked me to** help him.

### 3. Negatives — not to

- "Don't worry." → She **told me not to** worry.

> ⚠️ Typical error: *tell* and *ask* need an object (me, him, us): *He told me to wait* — not ~~he told to wait~~.

## Common mistakes

**1. Forgetting the object.** *~~She told to sit down~~* → **She told me to sit down.**

**2. Wrong negative form.** *~~He told me don't go~~* → **He told me not to go.**

**3. Using 'say' for commands.** *~~She said me to leave~~* → **She told me to leave. Pro tip:** command = tell + person + to; request = ask + person + to; negative = not to.
