## How to report a question

Use **statement word order**, no *do/does/did*, and no question mark.

| Direct question | Reported question |
|---|---|
| "Where **do you live**?" | She asked where **I lived**. |
| "What **is** your name?" | He asked what **my name was**. |
| "**Are you** ready?" | She asked **if I was** ready. |
| "**Do you** like it?" | He asked **whether I liked** it. |
| "Why **did she** leave?" | They asked why **she had left**. |

Keep the **wh-word** for wh-questions; use **if / whether** for yes/no questions.

## When and how to change things

### 1. Wh-questions — keep the question word

- "**Where** are you going?" → She asked **where** I was going.

### 2. Yes/no questions — use if / whether

- "**Do** you smoke?" → He asked **if** I smoked.

### 3. Drop 'do' and the question mark

- "What **does** he want?" → She asked what he **wanted**. (no *does*, no ?)

> ⚠️ Typical error: don't keep question word order. *She asked where I lived* — not ~~she asked where did I live~~.

## Common mistakes

**1. Keeping question word order.** *~~He asked what was my name~~* → **He asked what my name was.**

**2. Leaving in 'do/does/did'.** *~~She asked if did I like it~~* → **She asked if I liked it.**

**3. Adding a question mark.** *~~He asked where I lived?~~* → **He asked where I lived. Pro tip:** a reported question is really a statement about a question, so write it like a statement.
