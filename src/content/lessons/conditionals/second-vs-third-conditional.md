## The difference in one table

| | Second conditional | Third conditional |
|---|---|---|
| Time | Unreal present or future | Unreal past |
| If-clause | past simple | past perfect |
| Result | would + base verb | would have + past participle |
| Example | **If I had** money, **I'd travel**. | **If I had had** money, **I would have travelled**. |

The second imagines things being different **now**; the third imagines the **past** being different.

## When to use each

### Second — imagining now or in general

- **If I spoke** Japanese, **I'd apply** for the job. (I don't speak it now)

### Third — imagining a different past

- **If I had spoken** Japanese, **I would have got** that job. (I didn't, back then)

> ⚠️ The clue is often a time word: *now / today / these days* → second; *then / that day / last year* → third.

## Common mistakes

**1. Using the third when you mean now.** *~~If I had had a car, I'd have driven to work every day~~* (for a general present habit) → **If I had a car, I'd drive to work every day.**

**2. Using the second for a finished past.** *~~If I knew, I would have told you~~* → **If I had known, I would have told you.**

**3. Forgetting 'have' in the third.** *~~If I had studied, I would passed~~* → **If I had studied, I would have passed. Pro tip:** decide the time first — unreal now = second, unreal past = third.
