## Asking, giving and refusing permission

| Function | Words | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Ask (informal) | Can I…? | **Can I** sit here? |
| Ask (polite) | Could I…? | **Could I** borrow your pen? |
| Ask (formal) | May I…? | **May I** come in? |
| Give | You can / You may | **You can** go now. **You may** begin. |
| Refuse | You can't / You may not | **You can't** park here. |

## When to use each

### 1. Ask — choose the formality

- **Can I** have some water? (informal)
- **Could I** ask a question? (polite)
- **May I** leave the table? (formal)

### 2. Give permission — can / may

- Yes, **you can** borrow it. Students **may** use the library after 6.

### 3. Refuse — can't / may not

- Sorry, **you can't** go in there. Passengers **may not** smoke.

> ⚠️ Answer 'Could I…?' with **can**, not could: *"Could I leave early?" — "Yes, you can."* (not ~~you could~~).

## Common mistakes

**1. Giving permission with 'could'.** *~~Yes, you could~~* → **Yes, you can.**

**2. Adding 'to'.** *~~Can I to go?~~* → **Can I go?**

**3. Over-formal 'may' in casual speech.** *May I have a coffee?* in a café with friends → **Can I have a coffee?** is more natural. **Pro tip:** can = informal, could = polite ask, may = formal; give/refuse with can/may (not could).
