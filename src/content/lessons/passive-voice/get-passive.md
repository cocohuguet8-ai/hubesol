## The get passive

Swap **be** for **get** before the past participle. It sounds more informal.

| be passive | get passive |
|---|---|
| He **was injured** in the match. | He **got injured** in the match. |
| My bike **was stolen**. | My bike **got stolen**. |
| They **were paid** on Friday. | They **got paid** on Friday. |

Questions and negatives use **do**: *Did you **get paid**? I didn't **get paid**.*

## When to use it

### 1. Sudden or unwanted events

- She **got hurt** playing football. He **got fired** last week.

### 2. Changes of state and set phrases

- They **got married** in June. Hurry up and **get dressed**! I need to **get used to** the noise.

> ⚠️ The get passive is informal. In formal writing use *be*: *The samples **were tested*** (not ~~got tested~~).

## Common mistakes

**1. Mixing 'be' and 'get'.** *~~It was got broken~~* → **It got broken** (or **It was broken**).

**2. No 'do' in questions/negatives.** *~~Was he get hurt?~~* → **Did he get hurt?**

**3. Using it in formal writing.** *~~The results got published in the journal~~* → **The results were published in the journal. Pro tip:** get passive = informal, for things that happen to us; be passive = neutral and formal.
