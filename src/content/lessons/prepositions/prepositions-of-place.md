## at, on, in for place

Match the preposition to the **shape** of the place: a point, a surface, or an enclosed space.

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| at | a point or location | **at** the door, **at** the bus stop, **at** home |
| on | a surface or line | **on** the table, **on** the wall, **on** the bus |
| in | an enclosed space or area | **in** the box, **in** the kitchen, **in** London |

## When to use each

### 1. at — a specific point

- Meet me **at** the entrance. She's **at** work.

### 2. on — a surface or line

- The keys are **on** the desk. We sat **on** the floor. He's **on** the train.

### 3. in — inside a space or area

- The milk is **in** the fridge. They live **in** Italy.

> ⚠️ Fixed pairs to learn: **in** bed, **on** the bus/train/plane, **in** the car, **at** work/school, **on** the left.

## Common mistakes

**1. 'in' for a surface.** *~~in the wall~~* (a picture) → **on the wall.**

**2. 'on' for a vehicle you sit inside.** *~~on the car~~* → **in the car.**

**3. 'in home' instead of 'at home'.** *~~I'm in home~~* → **I'm at home. Pro tip:** at = a point, on = a surface/line, in = inside a space.
