## for, during, while

| Word | Followed by | Answers | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| for | a length of time | how long? | **for** two hours, **for** a week |
| during | a noun (event/period) | when? | **during** the meeting, **during** summer |
| while | a subject + verb (clause) | when? (two actions) | **while** I was cooking |

## When to use each

### 1. for — how long

- We waited **for** twenty minutes. She's lived here **for** ten years.

### 2. during — inside a period (+ noun)

- My phone rang **during** the film. It often rains **during** the night.

### 3. while — two things at once (+ clause)

- **While** I was reading, the baby fell asleep.

> ⚠️ 'During' takes a noun, not a length: *~~during three hours~~* → **for three hours**; *during the trip* is fine.

## Common mistakes

**1. 'during' + a length of time.** *~~I slept during eight hours~~* → **I slept for eight hours.**

**2. 'while' + a noun.** *~~while the meeting~~* → **during the meeting** (or **while the meeting was on**).

**3. 'for' for 'when'.** *~~It happened for the night~~* → **It happened during the night. Pro tip:** for = how long, during + noun, while + clause.
