## The difference at a glance

| | Past Simple | Past Perfect |
|---|---|---|
| When | the main past event | before another past event |
| Order | the later action | the earlier action |
| Example | I **got** home. | Everyone **had left**. |

The key question: which action came **first**? The earlier action takes the past perfect; the later action takes the past simple.

## Use the past simple for…

The sequence of main events.

- **I woke up, got dressed and left.**

> ⚠️ Typical error: ~~I had woken up, had got dressed and had left.~~ → For a simple sequence, the past simple is enough: *I woke up, got dressed and left.*

## Use the past perfect for…

The earlier action, to make the order clear.

- When I **woke up**, the sun **had** already **risen**.
- She **passed** the exam because she **had studied** hard.

> ⚠️ Typical error: ~~When I got to the station, the train left.~~ → The train left first, so use the past perfect: *the train had left.*

## Common mistakes

**1. Past simple for both when order matters.** *~~By the time we arrived, the film started~~* → **By the time we arrived, the film had started.**

**2. Past perfect for a simple sequence.** *~~She had opened the door and had walked in~~* → **She opened the door and walked in. Pro tip:** only reach for the past perfect when one action is clearly earlier than another.

**3. Wrong participle after 'had'.** *~~They had ate~~* → **They had eaten.**
