## How to form the future continuous

| Form | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Affirmative | subject + will be + verb-**ing** | She **will be working**. |
| Negative | subject + won't be + verb-ing | They **won't be coming**. |
| Question | Will + subject + be + verb-ing? | **Will** you **be joining** us? |

**Will be** is the same for every subject, and the main verb takes **-ing**. The verb *be* is never left out.

## When to use the future continuous

### 1. An action in progress at a future time

- At 8pm tonight, **I'll be watching** the match.
- This time next week, **we'll be relaxing** on the beach.

### 2. Polite questions about plans

- **Will you be needing** anything else?
- **Will you be using** the printer this afternoon?

> ⚠️ Typical error: ~~By six I'll be finishing.~~ (a completed action) → For something completed, use will or the future perfect: *I'll finish by six.*

## Common mistakes

**1. Using it for a completed future action.** The continuous is for an action in progress, not a finished one. *~~By ten I'll be finishing the report~~* → **By ten I'll have finished the report.**

**2. Leaving out 'be'.** *~~I will working at nine~~* → **I'll be working at nine.**

**3. Using it with stative verbs.** *~~I'll be knowing the answer soon~~* → **I'll know the answer soon. Pro tip:** know, want and like avoid the continuous.
