## How to form the past continuous

| Form | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Affirmative | subject + was/were + verb-**ing** | She **was working**. |
| Negative | subject + wasn't/weren't + verb-ing | They **weren't listening**. |
| Question | Was/Were + subject + verb-ing? | **Were** you **sleeping**? |

Use **was** with *I, he, she, it* and **were** with *you, we, they*. The main verb takes **-ing**, with the same spelling rules as the present continuous (*make → making, run → running*).

## When to use the past continuous

### 1. An action in progress at a past time

- At ten o'clock last night, **I was studying.**
- This time yesterday, **we were flying** to Rome.

### 2. A longer action interrupted by a shorter one

The long action is the past continuous; the short action that interrupts is the past simple.

- **I was having** a shower **when** the doorbell **rang**.

> ⚠️ Typical error: ~~I had a shower when the doorbell rang.~~ → The longer background action takes the past continuous: *I was having a shower when the doorbell rang.*

### 3. Two actions at the same time

Often with *while*.

- **While** she **was cooking**, he **was setting** the table.

## Common mistakes

**1. Past simple for the background action.** If an action was already in progress, use the past continuous. *~~I walked home when it started to rain~~* (background) → **I was walking home when it started to rain.**

**2. Past continuous with stative verbs.** State verbs use the past simple. *~~I was knowing the answer~~* → **I knew the answer. Pro tip:** know, want, like and understand avoid the continuous.

**3. Wrong was/were.** *~~You was sleeping~~* → **You were sleeping.** Was = I/he/she/it; were = you/we/they.
