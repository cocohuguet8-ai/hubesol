## How to form the future perfect

| Form | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Affirmative | subject + will have + past participle | She **will have left**. |
| Negative | subject + won't have + past participle | They **won't have finished**. |
| Question | Will + subject + have + past participle? | **Will** you **have arrived** by then? |

**Will have** is the same for every subject. The past participle is the verb + *-ed* for regular verbs, or the third form for [irregular verbs](/grammar/verb-tenses/irregular-verbs/).

## When to use the future perfect

### 1. Completed by a future point

Often with *by* or *by the time*.

- By Friday, **I'll have finished** the report.
- **She'll have graduated** by next summer.

### 2. With 'by the time'

- By the time you read this, **I'll have gone.**

> ⚠️ Typical error: ~~By 2030 I finish my studies.~~ → For something completed by a future point, use the future perfect: *By 2030 I'll have finished my studies.*

## Common mistakes

**1. Using the present simple for a completed future action.** *~~By June I finish the course~~* → **By June I'll have finished the course.**

**2. Wrong participle after 'will have'.** *~~will have went~~* → **will have gone.**

**3. Confusing it with the future continuous.** *~~By ten I'll be finishing~~* (in progress) → **By ten I'll have finished** (completed). **Pro tip:** future perfect = done by then; future continuous = in progress then.
