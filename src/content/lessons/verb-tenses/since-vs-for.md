## How to choose

| Word | Goes with | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **since** | a point in time (when it started) | since 9am, since 2019, since Monday, since I was a child |
| **for** | a length of time (how long) | for ten minutes, for three days, for years |

The quick test: can you replace it with a **number of hours/days/years**? Then it's *for*. Does it name **when something started**? Then it's *since*.

## Since = starting point

- I've known her **since** 2015.
- He's been ill **since** Monday.
- Everything has changed **since** we moved.

## For = duration

- We've lived here **for** five years.
- I waited **for** an hour.

> ⚠️ Typical error: ~~I've lived here since five years.~~ → 'Five years' is a length of time, so use *for*: **I've lived here for five years.**

## Common mistakes

**1. Swapping them.** *~~for 2019~~* → **since 2019**, and *~~since five years~~* → **for five years**.

**2. Using the wrong tense for a continuing situation.** *~~I live here for five years~~* → **I've lived here for five years. Pro tip:** since/for + a situation continuing now needs the present perfect.

**3. Using 'since' with a period.** *~~since three days~~* → **for three days** (or *since three days ago*).
