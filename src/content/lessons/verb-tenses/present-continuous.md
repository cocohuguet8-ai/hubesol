## How to form the present continuous

| Form | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Affirmative | subject + am/is/are + verb-**ing** | She **is working**. |
| Negative | subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing | They **aren't listening**. |
| Question | Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing? | **Are** you **coming**? |

Use **am** with *I*, **is** with *he/she/it*, and **are** with *you/we/they*. The main verb always takes **-ing**, and the verb *be* can never be left out.

## When to use the present continuous

### 1. Actions happening now

Something in progress at this moment.

- **She's talking** on the phone.
- Look! **It's raining.**

> ⚠️ Typical error: ~~Look! It rains.~~ → For something happening at this moment, use the present continuous: *It's raining.*

### 2. Temporary situations

True around now, but not permanent.

- **I'm reading** a great book at the moment.
- **He's living** in Madrid this year.

> ⚠️ Typical error: ~~I read a great book at the moment.~~ → A temporary current situation takes the present continuous: *I'm reading…*

### 3. Future arrangements

Fixed plans, usually with a time or place.

- **We're meeting** Sara tomorrow.
- **They're flying** to Rome on Friday.

## Spelling of the -ing form

| Rule | Verb ending | Example |
|---|---|---|
| add -ing | most verbs | working, playing |
| drop the -e, add -ing | verbs ending in *-e* | making, writing |
| double the consonant | short consonant–vowel–consonant verbs | running, sitting |

## Stative verbs

Some verbs describe a **state**, not an action — feelings, opinions, senses and possession. They are not normally used in the continuous: *know, like, love, want, need, believe, understand, prefer, own.*

- ~~I am wanting a coffee.~~ → **I want a coffee.**
- ~~She is knowing the answer.~~ → **She knows the answer.**

## Common mistakes

**1. Using the present continuous for habits.** A regular routine takes the present simple, not the continuous. *~~I'm going to the gym every day~~* → **I go to the gym every day.**

**2. Using stative verbs in the continuous.** State verbs (want, like, know, need) stay in the present simple. *~~I'm wanting a coffee~~* → **I want a coffee. Pro tip:** if it's a feeling, an opinion or possession — not an action you can watch — use the present simple.

**3. Leaving out the verb 'be'.** The present continuous always needs *am/is/are*. *~~She working now~~* → **She is working now.**
